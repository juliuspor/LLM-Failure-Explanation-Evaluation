What happened (where):
- The failure occurs in DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes when it validates the minutes parameter. See lines ~394–401 in your code:
  - It checks if minutes_offset < 0 or minutes_offset > 59 and raises ValueError("Minutes out of range: {minutes_offset}") (lines 400–401).
- Your test calls DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes(-2, -15). That passes minutes_offset = -15, so the guard trips and raises ValueError("Minutes out of range: -15") — exactly the observed exception.

Why it happened (root cause):
- The method is intended to accept combinations where the hour is negative and the minutes are also negative (e.g. (-2, -15) should mean -02:15). The Java Joda-Time logic in forOffsetHoursMinutes allows negative minutes when hours is negative by combining them into a total minutes value before range checking.
- In your Python translation you perform the range check for minutes_offset before you combine hours and minutes into a single signed minutes value. That check rejects -15 as an invalid minute value even though it should be permitted when the hours value is negative.

  a) allow negative minutes when hours_offset is negative (i.e. only enforce 0..59 when hours_offset >= 0), or
  b) be postponed until after you compute the combined signed minutes value and then validate the final magnitude (and that minutes component magnitude is <= 59).
