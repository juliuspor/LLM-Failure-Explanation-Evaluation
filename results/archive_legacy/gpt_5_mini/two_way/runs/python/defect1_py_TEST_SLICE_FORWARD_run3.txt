What happened (where)
- The bug is in hit01_timezone.py inside DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes, around the lines you showed (about line numbers 402–405).
- The problematic lines are these reassignments of minutes_offset:
  - line 402: minutes_offset = hours_in_minutes - minutes_offset
  - line 404: minutes_offset = hours_in_minutes + minutes_offset
- Because minutes_offset and hours_in_minutes are reused and overwritten incorrectly, the method computes the wrong total minutes (and thus wrong milliseconds offset and ID) for inputs where hours are negative or where signs must be combined carefully.

Why it fails (root cause)
- The code appears intended to combine hours_in_minutes (hours * 60) with an absolute minutes component, but instead it overwrites minutes_offset twice and uses hours_in_minutes on the right-hand side incorrectly.
- Concretely, suppose:
  - hours = -2 -> hours_in_minutes = -120
  - minutes (absolute) = 15 -> minutes_offset initially = 15
- Intended total minutes = -120 - 15 = -135 (-> -02:15)
- What the code actually does (given both lines present) ends up computing something else because the second assignment uses a minutes_offset value that has been overwritten by the first assignment. The meaning of the two lines is inconsistent and they do not implement a single correct branch for sign handling.
- Result: for (-2, -15) or (-2, 15) the function may produce the wrong minutes_offset (wrong sign or wrong magnitude), so get_offset(0) and get_id() do not match expectations. That explains failing tests like test_negative_hours_negative_minutes and test_mixed_signs_invalid.
