- The failure occurs here because ch3 and ch4 are integers (obtained with ord(...)) while the right-hand side ('A' and 'Z') are strings. In Python comparing an int to a str with < or > raises a TypeError (e.g. TypeError: '<' not supported between instances of 'int' and 'str').

Why it happened (root cause)
- The code mixed two different representations of characters:

- Python does not implicitly convert between ints and strings for relational comparisons, so the expression fails before the ValueError branch ever runs.
