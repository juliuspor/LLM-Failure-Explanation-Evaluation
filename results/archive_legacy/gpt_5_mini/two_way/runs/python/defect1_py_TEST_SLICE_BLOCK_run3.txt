What happened (where)
- In hit01_timezone.py inside DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes, at lines 396–397 you have:
  if minutes_offset < 0 or minutes_offset > 59:
      raise ValueError(f"Minutes out of range: {minutes_offset}")
- That code runs whenever you call DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes(...) and immediately rejects any negative minutes_offset.

Why it fails (root cause)
- The intent (and your tests / Joda-like behavior) is to accept minutes in the range -59 .. +59. But the condition minutes_offset < 0 or minutes_offset > 59 treats any negative minutes as out-of-range, so calls such as:
  - DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes(0, -15)
  - DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes(-2, -15)
  - DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes(-2, 15)
  are rejected at that check with ValueError even when they should be accepted (depending on sign rules).
- In short: the inequality is inverted. The code checks "minutes_offset < 0" as a reason to raise, when it should allow negative minutes down to -59.
