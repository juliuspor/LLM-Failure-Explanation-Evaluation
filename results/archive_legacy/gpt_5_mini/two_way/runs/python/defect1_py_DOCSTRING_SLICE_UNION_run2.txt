What happened (where)

      raise ValueError(...)
  This prevents any call that supplies a negative minutes_offset (e.g. (0, -15) or (-2, -15)), even though the docstring explicitly allows negative minutes in some combinations.
- At lines 402–405 the code then tries to combine hours and minutes by switching signs/adding/subtracting minutes:

  minutes_offset = hours_in_minutes + minutes_offset
  offset = FieldUtils.safe_multiply(minutes_offset, DateTimeConstants.MILLIS_PER_MINUTE)
  That logic is confusing and incorrect for the allowed sign combinations. It uses an absolute hours_in_minutes (positive) and then subtracts/ adds minutes_offset in a way that can flip the sign or produce the wrong total minutes. That leads to the later check at line 407 ("Offset is too large") or to producing the wrong offset sign/value.

Why it fails (root cause)
1. The minutes range check at line 396 enforces 0..59. The API allows minutes in -59..+59 for some combinations. Rejecting negative minutes causes valid inputs (per the docstring) to fail immediately.
2. The combination logic at 402–405 is wrong. Instead of computing a single signed total minute count from hours_offset and minutes_offset, it manipulates an absolute hours value and then adds/subtracts minutes in ways that:
   - produce the wrong sign when hours is negative and minutes is positive (e.g. (-2, 15) should become -135 minutes but the current arithmetic can yield +105 or other incorrect values),
   - or yield a magnitude that exceeds the allowed limit and triggers the "Offset is too large" error at line 407.
