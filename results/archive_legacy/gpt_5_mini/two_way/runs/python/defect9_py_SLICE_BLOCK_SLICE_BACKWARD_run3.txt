What went wrong (what happened and where)
- At line 52 you set search_length = len(search_list).
- At line 82 you index replacement_list[i] and search_list[i].
- The code fails because one of those index operations is out of range (IndexError). That happens when i >= len(replacement_list) or i >= len(search_list) during the loop that runs for i in range(search_length) at lines 59 and 81–83.

Why it happens (root cause)
- You used the length of search_list (search_length) to drive the loop, but replacement_list may be shorter than search_list. If replacement_list has fewer items than search_list, replacement_list[i] does not exist for some i.
- Alternatively (and commonly), you may have modified one of the lists earlier in the method (the loop starting at line 59 or other code) so that the original search_length is no longer valid by the time you reach the loop at line 81. If any code removes or shortens search_list or replacement_list after search_length was computed, an index that was valid when search_length was stored becomes invalid later.

Where it happened (specific lines)
- search_length assigned: line 52.
- first loop that may mutate lists: starts at line 59.
- failing access: line 82 (replacement_list[i]) and also line 82 (search_list[i] — either could raise).

Which to choose
- If the logic pairs items, use zip or min length (option 1).
- If replacements must exist for every search entry, validate lengths and raise (option 2).
- If mutation caused the mismatch, stop mutating in-place or iterate over a copy (option 3).
