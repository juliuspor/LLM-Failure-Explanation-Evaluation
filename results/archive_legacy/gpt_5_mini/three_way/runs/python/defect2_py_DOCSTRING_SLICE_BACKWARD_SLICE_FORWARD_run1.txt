What happened
- The code raises an exception at get_paint when computing g on line 71:
  g = int((value - self._lower_bound) / (self._upper_bound - self._lower_bound) * 255.0)
- If self._upper_bound == self._lower_bound the denominator (self._upper_bound - self._lower_bound) is zero, causing a ZeroDivisionError.

Why it happened (root cause)
- There is no check that the bounds are different. The constructor (__init__, lines 30–44) simply stores lower_bound and upper_bound without validating them, so callers can construct the object with equal bounds.
- get_paint assumes a nonzero range and performs a division by (upper_bound - lower_bound). When the range is zero, Python raises ZeroDivisionError before the later bounds check for g (lines 74–75) can run.

Where it occurs
- The division-by-zero occurs at line 71 inside get_paint.
- The subsequent check at lines 74–75 is never reached when the denominator is zero.
