What fails
- Python raises an indentation error at the line with "mantissa //= 10" (line 184).

Why it fails (root cause)
- The indentation of the "mantissa //= 10" line does not match the surrounding block. Python is whitespace-sensitive, so that single mis‑indented line inside the "if check_val == value:" block makes the parser fail.
- The error you will see is one of:
  - IndentationError: unexpected indent
  - IndentationError: unindent does not match any outer indentation level
  depending on whether the mismatch is an extra indent or inconsistent tabs/spaces.
- The specific location is line 184 in the slice you posted:
  - Lines 181–183 set up the while/if;
  - line 184 is intended to be the body of the "if check_val == value:" but its leading whitespace differs from the "exp += 1" line (line 185). That mismatch is what breaks the parser.

How to fix it (concrete steps)
1. Make the indentation consistent for that block. For example, use 4 spaces per indent and make lines 181–186 look like:
   - while mantissa % 10 == 0:
       check_val = (mantissa // 10) * (10 ** (exp + 1))
       if check_val == value:
           mantissa //= 10
           exp += 1
       else:
           break
2. Ensure there are no tabs mixed with spaces:
   - In your editor convert tabs to spaces (most editors offer "Convert tabs to spaces" or set tab width = 4 and "insert spaces").
   - Or run: expand -t 4 script.py > fixed.py (on Unix) or use an editor command.
3. Re-run the file; the IndentationError will be resolved. Optionally enable an editor rule or pre-commit hook to enforce consistent indentation going forward (PEP 8: 4 spaces per indent).

Notes
