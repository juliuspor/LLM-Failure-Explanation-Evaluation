What happened (root cause)
- At line 396 the code rejects any negative minutes:
  - if minutes_offset < 0 or minutes_offset > 59:
  - raise ValueError(f"Minutes out of range: {minutes_offset}")
- That single check prevents any negative minutes from ever reaching the later logic at lines 402 and 404, where the code intends to adjust minutes based on the sign of hours_offset.
- Because of that early rejection, valid cases described by the docstring are broken. For example:
  - (-2, -15) should produce -02:15 (allowed by the docstring), but is rejected at line 396.
  - (0, -15) should produce -00:15, but is rejected at line 396.
- The later checks (offset computation at line 405 and the “Offset is too large” raise at line 407) never get a chance to operate on negative minutes because the input is already refused.

Why this causes the failure
- The docstring allows minutes in the range -59..+59 and permits negative minutes when hours are zero or negative. The code’s validation enforces minutes in 0..59 only. That mismatch between documentation and implementation is the direct cause: correct inputs per the docstring trigger "Minutes out of range" at line 396 and fail early.
