The original to_locale assumed a two-letter country is always present when length>2, causing failure for strings like 'fr__POSIX' which have an empty country (double underscore). I will handle the double-underscore case explicitly and avoid indexing characters 3 and 4 unless they exist and are letters. The new logic: return None for None input; validate first two language chars; handle length==2; require '_' at pos2; if pos3 is '_' treat as empty country and set variant to substring from pos4; otherwise ensure at least two chars for country at pos3-4 and validate they are uppercase; handle optional variant that must be prefixed by '_' at pos5 if present.
