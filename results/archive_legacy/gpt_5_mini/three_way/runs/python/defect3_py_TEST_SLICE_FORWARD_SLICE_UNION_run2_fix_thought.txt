The bug is incorrect advancement of pos after translators consume codepoints. The translate method currently advances pos by char_count(code_point_at(code_units, pos)) inside a for loop per consumed. But that calls code_point_at after pos has advanced incorrectly leading to out-of-range. Instead, for each consumed translator we should inspect the codepoint at the original pos, determine char_count, and advance pos by that amount repeatedly. Better: compute consumed_units = 0; repeat consumed times: cp = code_point_at(code_units, pos); units = char_count(cp); pos += units; consumed_units += units. Alternatively simply loop consumed times and each time compute cp at current pos and advance accordingly. Ensure not to call code_point_at with out-of-range index. This requires checking pos < length before calling. Implement robust loop.
