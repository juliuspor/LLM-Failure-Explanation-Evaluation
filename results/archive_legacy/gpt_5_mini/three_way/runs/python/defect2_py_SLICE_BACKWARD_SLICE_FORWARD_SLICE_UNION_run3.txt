What failed
- The failure happens in get_paint at line 71:
  g = int((value - self._lower_bound) / (self._upper_bound - self._lower_bound) * 255.0)
- If self._upper_bound == self._lower_bound this performs a division by zero and raises a ZeroDivisionError.

Why it fails (root cause)
- The expression divides by (self._upper_bound - self._lower_bound). When those two bounds are equal the denominator is 0. Python raises ZeroDivisionError immediately; the code never reaches the later check at line 74 because the exception happens earlier in line 71.
- The check at lines 74–75 (if g < 0 or g > 255: raise ValueError(...)) cannot protect you from the division-by-zero because it runs after g is computed.
- Additional note: the ValueError message at line 75 uses an f-string but no interpolation; that’s harmless but likely not useful (it prints the literal text "Color parameter outside of expected range: Red Green Blue" instead of showing which value failed).
