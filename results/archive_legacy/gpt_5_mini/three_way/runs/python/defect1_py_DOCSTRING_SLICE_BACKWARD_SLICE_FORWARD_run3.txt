What happened (where it fails)
- At lines 396–397 the code unconditionally rejects any negative minutes:
  "if minutes_offset < 0 or minutes_offset > 59: raise ValueError(...)"
- That check runs before the code at lines 402 and 404 that adjusts minutes depending on the sign of hours.
- As a result, calls that should be allowed by the docstring are rejected early. Example: hours_offset = -2, minutes_offset = -15 should produce -02:15 (see doc table) but fails with ValueError at line 396 because minutes_offset is negative.

Why it fails (root cause)
- The check at lines 396–397 enforces minutes_offset to be non‑negative regardless of hours_offset.
- The code’s later logic (lines 402 and 404) expects to be able to accept negative minutes for zero or negative hour offsets and convert them into the correct total offset in minutes.
- In short: the minutes validation is too strict and happens in the wrong place relative to the sign-dependent adjustment logic.

Exactly where to change it (concrete fix)
- Replace the unconditional range test at lines 396–397 with a condition that allows negative minutes when hours_offset <= 0.
- One minimal, clear replacement:

  Replace:
    if minutes_offset < 0 or minutes_offset > 59:
        raise ValueError(f"Minutes out of range: {minutes_offset}")

  With:
    if minutes_offset > 59 or (minutes_offset < 0 and hours_offset > 0):
        raise ValueError(f"Minutes out of range: {minutes_offset}")

- This keeps the intended prohibition of negative minutes when hours_offset is positive (doc says (2, -15) is invalid), but permits negative minutes when hours_offset is zero or negative (so (-2, -15) and (0, -15) work). It also still rejects minutes > 59.

Additional safety notes
