What happened (where it fails)
- In get_paint (defined at line 54) you clamp the incoming value into variable v:
  - v = max(value, self._lower_bound)
  - v = min(v, self._upper_bound)
  Those two lines ensure v is within [self._lower_bound, self._upper_bound].
- But when you compute g at line 71 you use the original value, not the clamped v:
  - g = int((value - self._lower_bound) / (self._upper_bound - self._lower_bound) * 255.0)
  Because value may be outside the bounds, g can be <0 or >255 and triggers the ValueError at lines 74–75.

Why it happened (root cause)
- A simple variable mix-up: you intended to use the clamped value v but used value. That defeats the clamping and lets out-of-range inputs produce out-of-range g.

Other potential failure you should guard against
- If self._upper_bound == self._lower_bound the denominator in the g computation is zero and you will get a ZeroDivisionError at the same computation (line 71).

1. Use the clamped variable v when computing g:
