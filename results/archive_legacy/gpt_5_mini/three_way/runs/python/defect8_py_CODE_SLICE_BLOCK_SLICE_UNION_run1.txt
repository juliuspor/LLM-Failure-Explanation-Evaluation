The exact faulty line and why it's wrong
- Faulty expression (line numbers from your snippet):

- What that does:
  - It raises a ValueError for any length that is not 2 and not 5 and also is less than 7.
  - Concretely, it rejects lengths 3, 4 and 6 (because 3,4,6 are < 7 and are neither 2 nor 5).
  - It accepts lengths 2, 5 and any length >= 7.
- Why that is a problem:
  - The rest of the method assumes that when you leave that length-check block you either have length == 2 (returned earlier), length == 5, or length >= 7. If your intention was to accept 2, 5, and any length >= 7 then the boolean logic should express “length is not 2, not 5, and also less than 7 → invalid”. The current expression does express that, but it is fragile/ confusing and is probably not what you intended — the common mistake here is mixing up conjunctions/negations so that some lengths are wrongly classified.
  - If the intention was instead to allow only length == 2, length == 5, or length >= 7, the clearer and safer check is to explicitly fail when the length is not in the accepted set. As written, the condition is hard to read and easy to mis-evaluate when maintaining the code; that’s why you see failures later when slices or index accesses (ch3 = locale_str[3], ch4 = locale_str[4]) are performed on strings of unexpected length.
