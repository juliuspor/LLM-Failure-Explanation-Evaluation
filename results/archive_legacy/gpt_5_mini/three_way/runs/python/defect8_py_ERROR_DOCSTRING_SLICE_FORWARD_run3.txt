Why it failed (root cause)
- "fr__POSIX" uses the double-underscore form that means language="fr", country="" (empty), variant="POSIX".
- The parser assumes the country is always two characters at positions 3 and 4 (locale_str[3:5]). For "fr__POSIX" those characters are "_P" (an underscore and 'P'), not a valid uppercase country code.
- The parser then checks locale_str[5] expecting it to be '_' (the variant separator). For "fr__POSIX" index 5 is 'O', so the check fails and it raises ValueError(f"Invalid locale format: {locale_str}") — that is the observed error.
- In short: the code does not handle the allowed form where the country is empty (double underscore). It treats the second underscore as part of the country and later as the wrong character when checking for the variant separator.
