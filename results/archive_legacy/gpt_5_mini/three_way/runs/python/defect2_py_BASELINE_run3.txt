What happened (root cause)
- You correctly clamp into the local variable v on lines 66–67 (v = max(...); v = min(...)), but when you compute g you use the original parameter value instead of the clamped v:
  g = int((value - self._lower_bound) / (self._upper_bound - self._lower_bound) * 255.0)
- So for out-of-range inputs (e.g. value = -0.5 or 1.5) g is computed from the unclamped value and can fall outside 0–255. That triggers the ValueError test failure at the explicit range check on lines 74–75.

Where it occurs
- The clamping occurs at the two v assignments (lines shown in the slice block).
- The bad computation is at the g assignment (line shown as 71).
- The exception is raised at the range check immediately after (lines 74–75).

Why this causes the test to fail
- test_lower_bound_clamping calls get_paint(-0.5). v becomes 0.0 after clamping, but g is computed from value == -0.5, producing a negative g. The code then raises ValueError instead of returning (0, 0, 0).
