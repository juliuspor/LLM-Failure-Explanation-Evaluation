What happened (root cause)
- You correctly clamp the input into v on the first two lines, but on line 71 you compute g using the original value variable instead of the clamped v:
  - Line 66–68 (clamping): v = max(value, self._lower_bound); v = min(v, self._upper_bound)
  - Line 71 (bug): g = int((value - self._lower_bound) / (self._upper_bound - self._lower_bound) * 255.0)
- Because value can be outside the bounds (for example -0.5 in your test), the expression on line 71 produces a negative g (or a g > 255), so the check on line 74 triggers and raises the ValueError.

Why it fails for your test
- Test calls gps.get_paint(-0.5). Suppose self._lower_bound is 0.0:
  - After clamping, v becomes 0.0.
  - But line 71 uses value (-0.5), so (value - lower_bound) is -0.5 which gives g < 0.
  - The check on line 74 raises ValueError: Color parameter outside of expected range.

Where it occurred
- The incorrect variable is used at line 71 (the g calculation). The error is raised at line 74.
