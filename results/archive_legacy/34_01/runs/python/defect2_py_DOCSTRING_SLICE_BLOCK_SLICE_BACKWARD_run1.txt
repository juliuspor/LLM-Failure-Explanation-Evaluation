Why it fails (root causes)
1. Only g is validated. You compute/validate g but you never compute or check r and b (so they may be missing or incorrect later). The bounds check is incomplete and inconsistent with the docstring that promises an (r, g, b) tuple.
2. The exception message is wrong: the f-string has no placeholders, so you get the literal text "Red Green Blue" instead of telling the caller which variables/values were out of range. That makes debugging hard.
3. There is another potential failure not currently handled: if self._upper_bound == self._lower_bound, the division on line 71 will divide by zero and raise an exception before you even reach the range check.

- Validating all channels prevents a production bug where r or b are left unchecked.
- Using formatted values in the exception gives the caller precise information (which component and what value).
