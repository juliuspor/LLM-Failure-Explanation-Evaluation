What happened (where)
- At line 396 the code checks:
  if minutes_offset < 0 or minutes_offset > 59:
      raise ValueError(f"Minutes out of range: {minutes_offset}")
  This check runs before the code that adjusts a negative minute value at lines 402–404:
  402 |                 minutes_offset = hours_in_minutes - minutes_offset
  404 |                 minutes_offset = hours_in_minutes + minutes_offset
  After those adjustments the variable minutes_offset may be a valid positive total-minute count. Because the negative-minute validation runs too early, a valid input that encodes a negative hour with positive minutes (or vice versa) can produce minutes_offset == -15 at line 396 and raise ValueError: Minutes out of range: -15.

Why it failed (root cause)
- The code validates the raw minutes_offset value before converting/combining it with the hour component (hours_in_minutes). The early check treats a temporarily negative minutes_offset as an invalid value even though the later logic expects to transform that negative into a valid total offset. In short: validation is performed in the wrong place relative to the sign/combination logic.

Exact variables and lines involved
- minutes_offset at line 396 is checked and causes the exception.
- The corrective combination that would make minutes_offset valid happens at lines 402–404 (uses hours_in_minutes).
- The final offset used is assigned to offset and multiplied with FieldUtils.safe_multiply and DateTimeConstants.MILLIS_PER_MINUTE (line 405), then returned via cls.for_offset_millis(offset).

Concrete fixes (steps you can apply)
1) Move the minutes range check so it happens after the sign/combination logic:
   - Cut lines 396–397 and paste them after line 404 (i.e., after minutes_offset has been adjusted with hours_in_minutes).
   - This ensures you validate the final minutes_offset value, not the intermediate negative value.

OR

2) Validate using the absolute minutes portion before combination:
   - Replace the check with:
       if abs(minutes_offset) > 59:
           raise ValueError(f"Minutes out of range: {minutes_offset}")
   - This allows a negative minutes_offset that is within the 0–59 magnitude and lets the later combination succeed.

OR

3) Validate the minutes part separately from the combined minutes:
   - If you have a variable minutes_part (the parsed minutes before sign/combination), check:
       if minutes_part < 0 or minutes_part > 59:
           raise ValueError(f"Minutes out of range: {minutes_part}")
   - Keep the existing checks for the combined minutes_offset as appropriate after combination.

Verification steps after change
- Run unit tests that parse offsets with:
  - positive hours and positive minutes (e.g. +02:15)
  - negative hours and positive minutes (e.g. -02:15)
  - positive hours and negative minutes (if supported)
  - boundary minutes values 0, 59, and invalid like 60 and -60
- Confirm ValueError is raised only for truly out-of-range minute magnitudes, not for transient negative values used during combination.

That is the direct cause and three precise ways to fix it; moving the check after lines 402–404 is the simplest and least invasive.
