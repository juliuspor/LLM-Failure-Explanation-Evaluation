What happened
- The exception comes from the timezone-offset factory method DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(int hoursOffset, int minutesOffset).
- You called it with a positive hours value and a negative minutes value (for example: DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(2, -15)).
- The method explicitly rejects that combination and throws: ValueError: Minutes out of range: -15.

Why it fails (root cause)
- The factory enforces a rule about sign consistency between hours and minutes: when hours > 0, minutes must be 0..59; when hours < 0, minutes must be 0..59; when hours == 0, minutes may be negative (to produce a negative offset).
- A pair like (2, -15) is ambiguous under the API (should be either +02:15 or -02:15) and is therefore disallowed. The implementation contains a validation block at the start of DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(...) that checks the ranges and sign combinations and throws a ValueError when minutes < 0 and hours > 0 (this is where the "Minutes out of range: -15" message is produced).

Where in the code it happens
- In DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(int hoursOffset, int minutesOffset), in the validation/checks at the beginning of the method (the guard that validates hours in -23..+23 and minutes in -59..+59 and rejects inconsistent signs). That validation is responsible for the ValueError with the minutes value in the message.

How to fix it (concrete steps)
Pick one of these fixes depending on the intended offset:

1) If you intended a positive offset of 1 hour 45 minutes (i.e. +01:45) because 2 hours minus 15 minutes equals +1:45:
   - Replace DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(2, -15)
   - With DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(1, 45)

2) If you intended a negative offset of 2 hours 15 minutes (i.e. -02:15):
   - Replace DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(2, -15)
   - With DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(-2, 15)

3) If you construct offsets programmatically where sign might be mixed, compute a single signed minute total first and use the appropriate API:
   - totalMinutes = hoursOffset * 60 + minutesOffset
   - newHours = totalMinutes / 60  (integer division toward zero or floor depending on language)

   - Or use a millisecond-based factory if available (e.g., forOffsetMillis(totalMinutes * 60 * 1000)) so you avoid sign/part inconsistencies.

Why these fixes work
- They ensure the hours and minutes passed match the API’s required sign rules (no positive hours with negative minutes), or they convert the offset to a single signed quantity that the factory will accept.
