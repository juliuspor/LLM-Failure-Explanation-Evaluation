What happened (where it failed)
- The failure is thrown at the check in for_offset_hours_minutes (lines 400–401 in your slice):
  if minutes_offset < 0 or minutes_offset > 59:
      raise ValueError(f"Minutes out of range: {minutes_offset}")
- That means the method was called with a negative minutes_offset and the method rejected it with a ValueError.

Why it failed (root cause)
- The method mixes two different sign conventions for the minute parameter:
  - The validation at line 400 requires minutes_offset to be in the range 0..59 (i.e. non‑negative).
  - Callers commonly pass a negative minutes_offset to mean a negative offset (for example hours_offset = -1 and minutes_offset = -30 to mean -1:30). When those callers call this method the validation rejects the negative minutes and you get the ValueError.
- The code then reuses the minutes_offset parameter later (overwriting it with the total minutes). That reuse makes the intent ambiguous and hides whether minutes_offset was supposed to be signed or unsigned, which is why the two pieces of code disagree.
