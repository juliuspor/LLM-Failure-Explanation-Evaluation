What happened
- The error comes from the factory method that builds a fixed-offset zone: DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes (the validation at the start of that method throws ValueError).
- You called it with a positive hours value and a negative minutes value (for example: hours_offset = 2, minutes_offset = -15). The method rejects mixed signs like (2, -15) and raises "ValueError: Minutes out of range: -15".

Why it fails (root cause)
- The method requires the minutes value to have the same sign as the hours value (or be zero), except when hours == 0. In plain terms:
  - If hours > 0, minutes must be >= 0.
  - If hours < 0, minutes may be positive or negative (both represent a negative total offset).
  - If hours == 0, minutes may be positive or negative (to represent +00:15 or -00:15).
- Passing hours > 0 and minutes < 0 creates an ambiguous mixed-sign combination that the factory explicitly forbids; the validation code detects minutes < -59 or minutes < 0 when hours > 0 and raises that ValueError.

Where the check happens
- Inside DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes, at the top of the method where it validates the hours and minutes arguments. That is the line that checks minutes and raises ValueError ("Minutes out of range: ...") when the combination is not allowed.

How to fix it (concrete steps)
1. Decide the intended total offset (example intentions and correct calls):
   - If you meant +02:15, call: DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(2, 15)
   - If you meant +01:45 (because 2 hours minus 15 minutes = 1 hour 45 minutes), call: DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(1, 45)
   - If you meant -02:15, call either: DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(-2, 15) or DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(-2, -15) (both represent -02:15)
   - If you meant -00:15, call: DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(0, -15)

2. Change the sign of minutes to match hours, or fold the negative minutes into the hour value so minutes is non-negative when hours > 0. Concretely:
   - If you have (hours = H > 0, minutes = m < 0): set new_hours = H - 1 and new_minutes = 60 + m (example: (2, -15) -> (1, 45)).
   - Alternatively, if the intended total offset is negative, make hours negative and use non-negative minutes (example: want -2:15 -> (-2, 15)).

3. Re-run and the ValueError will go away once hours/minutes obey the method's rules.
