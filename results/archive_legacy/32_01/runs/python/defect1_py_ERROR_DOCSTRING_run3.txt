What happened
- You called the factory method that builds a fixed-offset time zone with a positive hours value and a negative minutes value.
- The method in Joda-Time is DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(int hours, int minutes). It validates that minutes have the same sign as hours (except the special case when hours == 0).
- Because you passed hours = 2 and minutes = -15 (or any positive hours with negative minutes), the method throws the validation error:
  ValueError (or IllegalArgumentException in Java) with message "Minutes out of range: -15".

Why it fails (root cause)
- The implementation explicitly forbids mixing signs: a positive hour and a negative minute is considered ambiguous and invalid. The docstring shows this case as "ValueError" for (2, -15).
- Internally the method checks the ranges and the sign relationship (minutes must be 0..59, and if hours > 0 then minutes must be >= 0). That check triggers and raises the exception, hence the "Minutes out of range: -15" message.

Where it occurs
- In DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(int hours, int minutes) — at the input validation branch that enforces:
  - hours in [-23, 23]
  - minutes in [-59, 59]
  - minutes must not be negative when hours > 0
  - resulting total offset must be within +/- 23:59:59.000
- The exception is raised at that validation point (the line that reports the minutes value in the error message).

How to fix it (concrete steps)
1. If you intended a positive offset of 2 hours and 15 minutes, pass a positive minutes value:
   - DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(2, 15)
2. If you intended an offset that is 2 hours minus 15 minutes (i.e. +1 hour 45 minutes), normalize the values before calling:
   - totalMinutes = hours * 60 + minutes
   - if totalMinutes outside [-1439, 1439] -> error (exceeds ±23:59)
   - normalizedHours = floorDiv(totalMinutes, 60)
   - normalizedMinutes = abs(totalMinutes % 60)
   - pass DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(normalizedHours, normalizedMinutes)
   Example: (2, -15) -> totalMinutes = 105 -> normalizedHours = 1, normalizedMinutes = 45 -> forOffsetHoursMinutes(1, 45)
3. If you meant a negative overall offset (e.g. -00:15), either pass hours = 0 and minutes negative, or pass hours negative and minutes positive according to the docstring cases:
   - DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(0, -15)  // -00:15
   - DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(-2, 15)  // -02:15

What to check in your code
- Find the call to DateTimeZone.forOffsetHoursMinutes(...) (or your equivalent factory) and inspect the sign of minutes when hours > 0.
- Replace the call with one of the corrected forms above or apply the normalization algorithm before calling.

This will prevent the "Minutes out of range: -15" exception and make the offset explicit and valid.
