The bug is in the final for loop of the translate method. The loop iterates `consumed` times (number of codepoints), but inside the loop it calls `char_count(code_point_at(code_units, pos))` each time, which recalculates the char count at the current (changing) position. This causes incorrect position advancement when consumed > 1. The correct approach is to get the char count once (for the codepoint at the current position) and multiply it by consumed to get the total advancement.
