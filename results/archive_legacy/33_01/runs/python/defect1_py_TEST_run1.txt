What happened (where it failed)
- The failure happens at these two lines:
  1) zone = DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes(-2, -15);
  2) assert zone.id == '-02:15'
- The assertion fails because zone.id is not '-02:15'. The code that constructs the zone receives a negative value for both hours and minutes and produces a different offset than you expected.

Why it failed (root cause)
- DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes expects the minutes argument to be a non‑negative number (0..59). The method applies the sign of the hours and then adds the minutes; giving a negative minutes value causes the sign/combination to be applied incorrectly and yields the wrong total offset.
- In short: passing -15 for the minutes parameter is incorrect. The API wants the minutes portion as a positive value and the sign should come from the hours parameter.

What actually happened (concrete result)
- At the assignment line zone = DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes(-2, -15); the constructed zone had an offset different from -2 hours 15 minutes. That is why the subsequent assert zone.id == '-02:15' fails.
