What happens
- At line 396 you reject any minutes_offset < 0 with:
  if minutes_offset < 0 or minutes_offset > 59:
      raise ValueError(f"Minutes out of range: {minutes_offset}")
- Later, at lines 402 and 404 you change minutes_offset relative to hours_in_minutes:
  402: minutes_offset = hours_in_minutes - minutes_offset
 404: minutes_offset = hours_in_minutes + minutes_offset
- That means you parse a negative time zone like "-05:30" so minutes_offset is parsed as -30, then the check at line 396 fires and raises ValueError before you ever apply the sign/normalize the minutes.

Why it fails (root cause)
- You validate minutes_offset before you normalize or apply the sign coming from the hours part.
- The validation treats a negative minutes value (which is legitimate when parsing "-05:30" as "-5 hours and -30 minutes") as an out-of-range error because the range check is done too early.
- In other words: the order is wrong. The code checks minutes_offset’s sign before the code at lines 402/404 uses hours_in_minutes (and the sign) to compute the combined offset.

Where it occurs
- The erroneous check is at line 396.
- The sign/combination logic that should happen before or instead of that check is at lines 402 and 404.
- The computed offset is then multiplied at line 405 and returned at line 408 via for_offset_millis.
