What happened (root cause)
- The function that raised ValueError was given a color described as the three names/variables "Red", "Green", "Blue" (or values with those names), but the library expected numeric RGB values inside a specific numeric range. Why this fails (concrete, simple)
- Many graphics APIs accept RGB as either:
  - integers 0–255 (Pillow, many image APIs), or
  - floats 0.0–1.0 (matplotlib color arrays), or
  - named color strings ("red") or hex strings ("#ff0000") — but not the literal tokens Red, Green, Blue or numbers outside the expected numeric range.
- If your variables Red, Green, Blue are:

  - integers outside the allowed bounds (e.g. 300 or -5), or
  - floats on the wrong scale (e.g. 255.0 passed where 0.0–1.0 is required),
  the library checks the values and throws ValueError.
