What happened (where it failed)
- The failing line is:

  assert zone.id == '-02:15'
- The test produced an offset id of "-01:45", not "-02:15", so the assertion failed.

Why it failed (root cause)
- DateTimeZone.for_offset_hours_minutes(int hours, int minutes) expects the minutes parameter to be non‑negative and uses the sign of the hours parameter to determine the overall sign of the offset.
- Internally the method uses something like:
    totalSeconds = hours * 3600 + (hours >= 0 ? minutes : -minutes) * 60
  (i.e. it flips the sign of minutes when hours < 0).
- With your call hours = -2 and minutes = -15 the method does:
    minutesUsed = -(-15) = 15
    total offset = -2 hours + 15 minutes = -1 hour 45 minutes
  which yields zone.id = "-01:45".
- In short: you passed a negative minutes value, but the method treats minutes as unsigned and applies the sign from hours again. That double-signing produced the wrong offset.
