What happened (root cause)
- The conditional on line 133 does not group the boolean operators the way you intended. In Python, and has higher precedence than or, so
  self.is_word_char(c) or c == '\\' and self.is_word_char(self.get_last_char())
is parsed as
  self.is_word_char(c) or (c == '\\' and self.is_word_char(self.get_last_char()))
- You intended to require that the first character of newcode is either a word character or a backslash, and also that the previous (last) character is a word character. In other words you meant:
  (self.is_word_char(c) or c == '\\') and self.is_word_char(self.get_last_char())
- Because of the actual parsing, whenever self.is_word_char(c) is True (for example c == '0'), the whole condition becomes True regardless of what get_last_char() returned. That makes add() insert a space before digits even when the previous character was a minus sign ('-'), producing "- 0.0" in the generated output.
- The downstream consumer of that output (your test or parser) ended up interpreting the space-separated tokens such that the unary minus was not applied to the numeric literal, so the produced value was 0 instead of -0.0. That is why the assertion failed: Expected -0.0, got 0.
