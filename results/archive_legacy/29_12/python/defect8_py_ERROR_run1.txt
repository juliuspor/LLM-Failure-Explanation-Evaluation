Why "fr__POSIX" is invalid (concrete details)
- Locale names normally follow a pattern like:
  - language (e.g. "fr")
  - optional territory separated by underscore (e.g. "fr_FR")
  - optional encoding separated by dot (e.g. "fr_FR.UTF-8")
  - optional modifier/variant separated by @ (e.g. "fr_FR@POSIX")
- "fr__POSIX" has two underscores with nothing between them, which indicates an empty territory. The parser you used does not accept an empty territory token and also does not treat the second underscore as the correct separator for a modifier. That is why the parser throws "Invalid locale format".
