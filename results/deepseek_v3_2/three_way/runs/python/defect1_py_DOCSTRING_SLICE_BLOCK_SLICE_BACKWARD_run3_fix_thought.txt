The bug was that minutes_offset validation incorrectly rejected negative values. Negative minutes are valid for offsets like -05:30. The fix is to allow minutes_offset in range -59 to 59, but ensure that hours_offset and minutes_offset have the same sign (both non-negative or both non-positive). Also, the calculation of total minutes must correctly combine hours and minutes with their signs. The original code attempted to adjust minutes_offset based on hours_in_minutes sign, which was flawed. Instead, we compute total_minutes = hours_offset * 60 + minutes_offset directly after validating sign consistency. This yields the correct total offset in minutes, which is then converted to milliseconds.
