The bug was that the function `char_at` was called with an index that could be out of bounds for single-character strings like 'l' or 'L', causing an IndexError instead of the expected ValueError. The fix replaces the `char_at` call with direct string indexing after checking the length, ensuring proper bounds and consistent error handling. Specifically, in the condition for 'L' suffix, we now check `len(numeric) > 0` before accessing `numeric[0]` to avoid IndexError and raise ValueError for invalid inputs.
