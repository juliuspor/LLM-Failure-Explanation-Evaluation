The bug was that the original code reassigned the `minutes_offset` variable to a new value that could be out of the valid range (0-59). The fix is to compute the total minutes directly without reassigning the input parameter, ensuring the total minutes are within the valid range for the final offset calculation. The validation of hours and minutes remains the same, but the calculation is simplified to avoid modifying the input variable. The total minutes can range from -23*60 - 59 to +23*60 + 59, which is within the allowed offset range of -23:59:59.999 to +23:59:59.999 in milliseconds. The safe_multiply will handle overflow.
