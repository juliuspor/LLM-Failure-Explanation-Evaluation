The bug diagnosis incorrectly points to line 38, but the actual issue is that the loop in `translate` can call `code_point_at` with an index that may be out of bounds after consuming surrogate pairs. Specifically, after `translate_codepoint` returns a non‑zero `consumed`, the inner `for` loop increments `pos` by calling `char_count(code_point_at(...))` on the current `pos`. However, if `consumed` is greater than 1, the second iteration of the `for` loop will call `code_point_at` with an index that has already been advanced beyond the length of `code_units`. The fix is to compute the total advance before the loop and then add it to `pos` in one step, avoiding repeated out‑of‑range accesses.
