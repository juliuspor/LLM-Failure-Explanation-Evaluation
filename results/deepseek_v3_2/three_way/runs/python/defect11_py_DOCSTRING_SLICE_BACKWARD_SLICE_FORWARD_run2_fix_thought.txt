The bug is in the `_next_int_java` helper function, which expects a `bound` parameter, but the caller `_random_internal` passes `gap` (computed as `end - start`). However, the diagnosis incorrectly points to `RandomStringUtils.random` function. Actually, the issue is that `_next_int_java` is called with `gap`, which could be zero or negative if `start` >= `end`. This triggers the ValueError. The fix is to ensure `gap` is positive before calling `_next_int_java`. Also, the function signature of `RandomStringUtils.random` is correct, but the internal logic must validate `start` and `end` to ensure `gap > 0`.
