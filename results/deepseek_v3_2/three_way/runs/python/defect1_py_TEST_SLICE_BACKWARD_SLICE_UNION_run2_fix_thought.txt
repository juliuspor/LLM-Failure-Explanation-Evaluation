The bug was that the validation for minutes_offset only allowed 0-59, but the method should allow minutes in [-59, 59] to support mixed signs (e.g., -2 hours and +15 minutes). The fix expands the range to -59..59. Also, the calculation of total minutes was incorrect: it was overwriting minutes_offset with hours_in_minutes plus/minutes minutes_offset, which didn't handle negative minutes correctly. Instead, compute total_minutes = hours_offset * 60 + minutes_offset directly, which correctly combines signs. This matches the documented behavior and passes the test.
