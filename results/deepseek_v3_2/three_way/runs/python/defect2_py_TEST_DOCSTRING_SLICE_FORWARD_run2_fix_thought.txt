The bug is that the get_paint method uses the original 'value' in the interpolation formula instead of the clamped value 'v'. After clamping, we should compute 'g' based on 'v', not 'value'. This ensures that values below lower_bound are clamped to lower_bound, resulting in g=0, and values above upper_bound are clamped to upper_bound, resulting in g=255. The fix is to replace 'value' with 'v' in the calculation of 'g'.
