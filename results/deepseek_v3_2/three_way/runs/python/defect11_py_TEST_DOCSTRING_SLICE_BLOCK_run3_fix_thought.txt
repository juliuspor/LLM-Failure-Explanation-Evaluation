The bug is that the method `random` does not validate the `start` and `end` parameters to ensure `start < end` as required by the half-open interval `[start, end)`. The test expects a `ValueError` with a message containing both 'start' and 'end'. The fix adds a check at the beginning of the method to raise `ValueError("start must be less than end")` when `start >= end`. This ensures the error message includes the required substrings and prevents invalid ranges from being passed to the internal worker.
