The bug is that when `start` and `end` are equal (or `end <= start`), the internal `gap` becomes zero or negative, causing `_next_int_java` to raise a generic 'bound must be positive' error. To meet the test expectation, we need to validate `start` and `end` directly in the `random` method, raising a ValueError that mentions both parameters. The fix adds a check at the beginning of the method, ensuring the error message includes 'start' and 'end' as required.
