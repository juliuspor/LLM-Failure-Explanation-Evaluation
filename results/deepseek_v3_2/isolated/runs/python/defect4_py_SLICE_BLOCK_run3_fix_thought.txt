The bug diagnosis incorrectly states that `_min_start_index` is never defined. Actually, it is defined in the `__init__` method of `TimePeriodValues`. The real bug is in `_update_bounds` where `self._max_middle_index` incorrectly references `self._min_middle_index` when fetching the period for comparison. This causes wrong comparisons for max middle index. The fix is to change `self.get_data_item(self._min_middle_index)` to `self.get_data_item(self._max_middle_index)` on line 335.
