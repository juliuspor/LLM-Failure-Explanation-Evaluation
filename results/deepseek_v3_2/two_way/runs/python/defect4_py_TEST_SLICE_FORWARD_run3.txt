The test fails because the `_update_bounds` method in `TimePeriodValues` incorrectly uses the variable `i` (the loop index) instead of `max_middle_index` when updating the maximum middle timestamp. In the provided test `test_max_middle_index_simple`, the code adds four time periods. The middle timestamps are 50, 150, 25, and 75. The maximum middle is 150 at index 1. However, the bug causes the method to compare the current middle timestamp with the middle timestamp at index `i` (which is the loop counter) rather than the middle timestamp at the stored `max_middle_index`. This leads to an incorrect comparison and the wrong index being set. Specifically, when the loop reaches index 3 (middle=75), it compares 75 with the middle at index `i` (which is also 75) instead of comparing with the middle at the current `max_middle_index` (which should be 150 at index 1). This results in `max_middle_index` being incorrectly updated to 3. The fix is to change the comparison to use the middle timestamp at `max_middle_index`.
