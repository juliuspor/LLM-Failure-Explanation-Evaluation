The bug diagnosis incorrectly states that `_min_start_index` is never defined. Actually, the `__init__` method of `TimePeriodValues` does define `_min_start_index` and other indices, initializing them to -1. However, there is a bug in `_update_bounds`: when updating `_max_middle_index`, it incorrectly references `self._min_middle_index` instead of `self._max_middle_index` to get `max_middle_period`. This leads to using the wrong period for comparison, causing incorrect bounds. The fix is to change `self.get_data_item(self._min_middle_index)` to `self.get_data_item(self._max_middle_index)` in the `_max_middle_index` section.
