The bug is that the method uses the original `value` in the interpolation formula instead of the clamped value `v`. After clamping, we should compute `g` based on `v`. This ensures that values below the lower bound are clamped to the lower bound, resulting in 0 (black), and values above the upper bound are clamped to the upper bound, resulting in 255 (white). The fix is to replace `value` with `v` in the calculation of `g`.
