The bug is that the `random` method does not validate its inputs before calling `_random_internal`. The internal method does check `count` and `chars`, but the `gap` (end - start) may be zero or negative, leading to a non-positive bound in `_next_int_java`. The fix adds validation for `count` (zero returns empty string, negative raises error) and ensures `end > start` to guarantee a positive gap. This matches the Java behavior where `IllegalArgumentException` is thrown for invalid ranges. The validation is placed in the public method to provide clear error messages and avoid internal errors.
