The bug is in the `translate` method's while loop: after consuming codepoints via `translate_codepoint`, the loop uses `char_count(code_point_at(code_units, pos))` to advance `pos`. However, `code_point_at` is called with `pos` that may be at the end of `code_units` (i.e., `pos == length`), causing an IndexError. The fix is to check `pos < length` before calling `code_point_at` in the inner for-loop, or restructure the loop to avoid the out-of-bounds access.
