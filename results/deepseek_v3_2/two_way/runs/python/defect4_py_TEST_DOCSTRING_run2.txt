The test fails because the `_update_bounds` method incorrectly uses the variable `max_middle_index` when comparing the middle timestamp of the newly added period against the current maximum middle timestamp. Instead, it should compare against the middle timestamp of the period at the current `max_middle_index`. The bug is in the line where `self._max_middle_index` is updated: it compares `middle` with `self._max_middle_index` (an integer index) rather than with the middle timestamp at that index. This leads to incorrect index assignment, causing `get_max_middle_index()` to return a wrong value (e.g., 3 instead of 1 in the test). The fix is to retrieve the middle timestamp at `self._max_middle_index` and compare `middle` against that timestamp.
