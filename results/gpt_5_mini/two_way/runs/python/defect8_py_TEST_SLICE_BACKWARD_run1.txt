The code checks characters at fixed positions (3 and 4) without accounting for the language__variant case where the country is empty and the format is language__variant (e.g. "fr__POSIX"). At slice lines 227-229 it reads locale_str[3] and locale_str[4] and then enforces they are uppercase letters; for inputs with double underscore the character at position 3 is '_' not an uppercase letter, so the condition fails and a ValueError is raised. In short: to_locale assumes a non-empty 2-char country when parsing the 5-character pattern and therefore misinterprets language__variant (empty country) strings, causing an incorrect "Invalid locale format" error.
