The bug is in the block that computes the maximum middle index: it reads the period from self._min_middle_index instead of self._max_middle_index. Specifically, at the line that sets max_middle_period = self.get_data_item(self._min_middle_index).get_period() (inside the if self._max_middle_index >= 0: branch). Because of this copy/paste error, max_middle is computed from the wrong item; if _min_middle_index and _max_middle_index differ, comparisons with middle will be incorrect and _max_middle_index may never be updated or may be updated incorrectly. This occurs in the _update_bounds method when updating the max-middle tracking (the fourth bounds block). The failure manifests as wrong/unstable _max_middle_index after inserting items. Fix: change that call to use self._max_middle_index. Also review similar copy/paste errors in other blocks.
