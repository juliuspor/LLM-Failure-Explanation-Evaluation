The code incorrectly adjusts minutes_offset when hours_offset is negative. At slice lines 383 and 385 it assigns minutes_offset = hours_in_minutes - minutes_offset for negative hours and minutes_offset = hours_in_minutes + minutes_offset for positive hours, but hours_in_minutes already represents hours_offset * 60; for negative hours this computes an incorrect total minutes sign. Specifically: if hours_offset = -1 and minutes_offset = 30, hours_in_minutes = -60; line 383 yields minutes_offset = -60 - 30 = -90, producing -90 minutes (-1h30m) instead of the intended -30 minutes (-0h30m) or combined -90? The intended behavior is to combine sign correctly: total_minutes = hours_in_minutes - minutes_offset only when the overall offset should be more negative, but current logic double-applies sign and yields offsets outside allowed range. This leads to FieldUtils.safe_multiply producing an offset too large and raising ValueError("Offset is too large") at line 388 or incorrect DateTimeZone returned at line 389. Root cause: incorrect handling of sign between hours_offset and minutes_offset; minutes_offset should be added to hours_in_minutes with the same sign as hours_offset (i.e., total = hours_in_minutes + (hours_offset < 0 ? -minutes_offset : minutes_offset)), and range checks adjusted accordingly. Refer to variables: hours_offset, minutes_offset, hours_in_minutes, offset, and lines 377-389.
