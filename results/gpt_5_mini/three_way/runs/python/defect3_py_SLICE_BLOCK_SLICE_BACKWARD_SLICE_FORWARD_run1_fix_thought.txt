The bug described is that code_point_at was checking against an uninitialized local code_units in to_utf16_code_units — but actually the diagnosis indicates to_utf16_code_units left code_units empty; however the fix requested is for CharSequenceTranslator.translate. The real issue in translate is the loop advancing pos after a consumed >0: it uses for pt in range(consumed): pos += char_count(code_point_at(code_units, pos)) which calls code_point_at with pos while pos may be out of bounds after increment. Safer to compute consumed codepoints by advancing by codepoint lengths without calling code_point_at repeatedly; instead, iterate consumed times and advance pos by char_count(code_point_at(code_units, pos)) but ensure pos < length. Alternatively, when a translator returns N (number of codepoints consumed), advance pos by the number of UTF-16 code units corresponding to those N codepoints; we can loop N times and use code_point_at but checking bounds.
