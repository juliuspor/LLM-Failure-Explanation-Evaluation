The bug arises because after a translator consumes codepoints it advances pos by counting char_count(code_point_at(code_units, pos)) inside a loop, but that calls code_point_at at the same pos repeatedly while pos is advanced causing when a surrogate pair consumed it might call code_point_at on the low surrogate. Fix by using a local variable to fetch codepoint once per iteration, increment pos by char_count(codepoint) rather than recomputing and avoid calling code_point_at after pos advanced. Simpler: when consumed>0, advance pos by sum of char_count for each consumed codepoint by reading code_point_at at current pos then incrementing pos by char_count(codepoint) in loop consuming times; do not call code_point_at with pos that equals low surrogate. Implement safe logic: for _ in range(consumed): cp = code_point_at(code_units, pos); pos += char_count(cp).
