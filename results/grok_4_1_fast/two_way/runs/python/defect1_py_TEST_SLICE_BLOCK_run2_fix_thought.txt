The bug is in the minutes validation which incorrectly rejects positive minutes (15) for negative hours. The validation `minutes_offset < 0 or minutes_offset > 59` passes for 15 (since 15 >= 0 and 15 <= 59), so the error occurs later in the offset calculation logic where `hours_in_minutes - minutes_offset` for negative hours produces incorrect results. The fix properly calculates total minutes as `hours_offset * 60 + minutes_offset` (preserving sign), then converts to millis.
