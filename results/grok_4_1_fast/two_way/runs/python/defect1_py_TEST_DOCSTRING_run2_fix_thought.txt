The bug is in the incorrect handling of negative hours with positive minutes in `for_offset_hours_minutes`. The current logic wrongly treats (-2, 15) as invalid by subtracting positive minutes from negative hours_in_minutes, leading to incorrect total minutes and potential overflow detection. The fix computes total minutes as hours_offset * 60 + minutes_offset (preserving input signs), then converts to millis using safe_multiply, allowing valid mixed-sign inputs like (-2, 15) = -105 minutes = -02:15.
