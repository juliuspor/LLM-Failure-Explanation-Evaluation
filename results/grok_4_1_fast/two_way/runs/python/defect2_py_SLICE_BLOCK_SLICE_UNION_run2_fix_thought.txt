The bug occurs because the clamping of `value` to bounds is ignored in the `g` calculation, which uses the original `value` instead of the clamped `v`. This causes `g` to be computed outside [0,255] for out-of-bounds inputs. Fix by using the clamped `v` in the calculation, ensuring `g` is always in [0,255] after `int()` truncation, making the bounds check redundant (but kept for safety).
