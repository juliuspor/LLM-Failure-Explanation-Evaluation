However, `int(255.0)` produces 255 due to floating-point precision, but the condition `g > 255` can falsely trigger if floating-point arithmetic yields slightly more than 255.0 (e.g., 255.0000001), causing `int()` to clamp to 255 yet still fail the check since 255 > 255 is False but any tiny excess before int() triggers the error path.
