The test `test_negative_zero` in `TestCodeConsumerAddNumber` fails because `CodeConsumer.add_number(-0.0)` outputs "0" instead of the expected "-0.0". Python's `str(-0.0)` and `repr(-0.0)` both return "0.0", losing the negative zero sign. The `is_negative_zero` utility correctly detects negative zero (as shown by passing `TestNegativeZeroUtility`), but the `add_number` method in `hit06_codeconsumer.py` fails to use this utility to specially format negative zero as "-0.0".
