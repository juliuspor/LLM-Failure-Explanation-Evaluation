The bug occurs because when `chars` is provided, `_random_internal` incorrectly sets `start=0` and `gap=len(chars)`, causing `_next_int_java(rnd, gap)` to be called with `bound=len(chars)` but the diagnosis indicates slicing fails with start=0. The fix ensures `start` is always positive by using `start=1` when chars are provided, making `idx = 1 + _next_int_java(rnd, len(chars)-1)` to access `chars[1:len(chars)]` range correctly.
