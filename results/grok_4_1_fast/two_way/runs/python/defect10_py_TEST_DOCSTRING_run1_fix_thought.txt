The bug occurs because when `last_char` is not a digit (e.g., 'l', 'F', 'junk'), the non-numeric suffix handling block executes but fails to raise ValueError for invalid inputs like 'l' or 'bobL'. The `is_digits` checks pass incorrectly for these cases, and exceptions aren't always raised. Fixed by adding early validation to ensure the numeric part is valid before attempting conversions, and ensuring ValueError is raised for all invalid cases as per the docstring.
