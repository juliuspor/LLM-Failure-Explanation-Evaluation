The diagnosis is incorrect - the IndexError check in `code_point_at` is not the issue as `code_units` is populated from `input_seq` and `index` is `pos` from the loop. The real bug is in the translate method's advancement logic: after `translate_codepoint` consumes characters, it incorrectly advances `pos` by summing `char_count` for each consumed codepoint starting from the already-consumed position, causing double-skipping and missing characters. Fixed by simply advancing `pos` by the number of code units consumed.
