The bug diagnosis points to an error in `to_utf16_code_units` at line 36, but that's not in `translate`. The real issue in `translate` is in the loop after translation: `code_point_at(code_units, pos)` is called with an unmodified `pos` before advancing it with `char_count(code_point_at(code_units, pos))`, causing `pos` to reference invalid indices (like 2 in an empty list scenario). Fixed by computing the codepoint once and advancing `pos` by the correct UTF-16 length.
