The bug occurs because `random.randrange(bound)` in `_next_int_java` is called with `gap = end - start`, but the test passes invalid range `start=5, end=5` (gap=0). Python's `randrange(0)` raises ValueError('bound must be positive') before the custom check in `_next_int_java`. The fix adds validation in `random` method to catch `start >= end` early with a custom message mentioning 'start' and 'end', matching test expectations.
