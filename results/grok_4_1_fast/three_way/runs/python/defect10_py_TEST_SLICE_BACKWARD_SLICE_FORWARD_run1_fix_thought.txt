The bug occurs because `char_at(numeric, 0)` is called on 'bob' when checking 'bobL', but `numeric` is constructed incorrectly due to malformed `exp` slicing when there's no exponent. For invalid inputs like 'bobL', `exp_pos` is -1, causing `exp = val[exp_pos + 1 : len(val) - 1]` to slice with negative start index, leading to short `numeric` and IndexError in `char_at`. Fix by adding early validation using `is_number` before complex parsing, ensuring only valid numbers proceed to slicing operations.
