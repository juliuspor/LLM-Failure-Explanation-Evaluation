The code fails due to a bug in the `_max_middle_index` update block. On the line `max_middle_period = self.get_data_item(self._min_middle_index).get_period()`, it incorrectly fetches the period using `self._min_middle_index` instead of `self._max_middle_index`. This means it compares the new item's middle timestamp against the middle of the item at the current minimum middle index, not the maximum middle index. As a result, the `_max_middle_index` is updated based on the wrong reference value, causing incorrect tracking of the maximum middle timestamp index.
